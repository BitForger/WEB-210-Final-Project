Multiplayer Gameplay

So your ready for the big leagues? Well let's go! Multiplayer Minecraft is a whole different dynamic of the game that happens to be a personal favorite. Here you can connect to servers around the world and play with hundreds to thousands of other players, or keep it small with your own LAN party of up to 8 people.

To get into multiplayer mode click the 'Multiplayer' button on the homescreen of the game. There you will go into a server select screen. There are thousands of Minecraft servers out there, but my two favorites are Cubecraft Games and Nemesis Gaming.

Computers hosting on your LAN (Local Area Netowork) are also shown there underneath your server list. This is where you can join in with friends and family on your home network. This can be a particularly entertaining way to play especially with custom modded clients.

Setting Up a LAN Game

To start setting up the LAN game hit the ESC key to get to the pause menu. On that screen you should see a button that says 'Open To LAN', click that.

It opens up to a settings screen where you can adjust the gameplay for your friends. In Minecraft 1.8 a spectator mode was added to only allow people in to watch what you are doing. Adding cheats is also an available option.

All you have to do next is click 'Start LAN World' and the game will start hosting on your IP address and the port it lists in the chat log. Now your friends can see your game in the multiplayer menu and can join you in whatever your crafting exploits happen to be!
